Moroccan Social Innovation Awards

Visual Identity & Design Development — Case Study

Overview

The Innovation Recognition Challenge — What one ought to learn is how to celebrate innovation while honoring tradition. This project was built upon the creation of meaningful, culturally-rooted recognition systems for Moroccan social entrepreneurs. The design moves beyond generic awards and forms an identity that reflects national progress and social impact.

The Social Innovation Context

In 2023 the Moroccan Ministry of Solidarity commissioned a full identity system for a national recognition program. With the rise of social entrepreneurship, a unified award system was needed—one that communicates credibility while inspiring the new generation of innovators shaping Morocco's social landscape.

The Generic Award Problem

Most existing award templates felt corporate — visually disconnected from creative Moroccan innovation. Recognition that celebrates social impact must feel human, grounded in community values, and visually capable of carrying cultural meaning.

The Innovation-Centered Solution

A modular identity built for flexibility and scale. A system that celebrates individual innovators while maintaining national recognition consistency. Modern design, traditional Morocco, one unified visual language.

Design Development

Using Adobe Creative Suite, visual research led to culturally-symbolic assets, bilingual typography, and geometric structures inspired by Moroccan heritage. The visual voice needed to feel modern—yet rooted in centuries of cultural meaning.

Understanding Moroccan Design

  • Mathematical geometric patterns → growth & balance
  • Cultural color symbolism → strength, nature, spirituality
  • Typography compatible with Arabic + French
  • Symbolism representing collaboration & social impact

Modular Pattern System

Scalable patterns, adaptable color systems, and layouts designed for print + digital media. This modularity made the system highly responsive for platforms like Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and ceremonial environments.

System Components

  • 24 traditional geometric digital patterns
  • 8 cultural color codes optimized for screens
  • Typography supporting Arabic — French — Amazigh
  • 12 social media layout templates
  • Documentation for cultural authenticity
